# Plumbob — The Challenge Book (Detailed Edition)

**130 Sims 4 challenges, each written as a full guide — backstory, setup with cheats, rules, strategy, scoring and variations.**

This is the deep version of the catalogue. Every challenge is written to the same standard as the best community write-ups: a premise that sells the fantasy, exact setup instructions (including the cheats to type), a split between **general rules** that always apply and **specific rules** unique to the challenge, **strategy tips** to actually get you through it, optional **scoring**, **want-it-harder** variants, a **milestone** checklist, and **variations** for replay. Established community classics carry their real, credited rulesets; original Plumbob challenges are built to the same depth.

### How difficulty works

Difficulty is rated in plumbobs (◆) on a 1–5 scale.

| Rating | Tier | What to expect |
|:--|:--|:--|
| ◆◇◇◇◇ | **Casual (1/5)** | Light constraints. Good for a relaxed session or a newer player. |
| ◆◆◇◇◇ | **Moderate (2/5)** | Real rules to follow, but forgiving. |
| ◆◆◆◇◇ | **Hard (3/5)** | Demands planning and consistency; mistakes cost you. |
| ◆◆◆◆◇ | **Brutal (4/5)** | Punishing, with little margin for error. |
| ◆◆◆◆◆ | **Extreme (5/5)** | Long, harsh, or both — a true test of mastery. |

### The cheats you'll actually use

Most challenges only ever need these, and only at setup. Open the console with **Ctrl + Shift + C**.

- `testingcheats true` — enables the cheat system (type this first).
- `money 0` or `money 1800` — sets your household funds to an exact figure for a fair start.
- `freerealestate on` — makes lots free, used once to place your founder, then turned off.
- `bb.moveobjects on` — build-mode freedom for placing objects; never a gameplay advantage.

Anything beyond these (motherlode, kaching, career or skill cheats) is considered a fail in most progression and legacy challenges unless a rule says otherwise.

## 1. Wealth & Progression

*The classic Sims power fantasy: start with empty pockets and an empty lot, and earn every single Simoleon the hard way. These runs are about the climb — no inheritances, no shortcuts, just skills, hustle and discipline turning nothing into a fortune. They're the perfect cure for the "I have ten million Simoleons and nothing to do" boredom that hits most saves eventually.*

### 1. Rags to Riches

*Your Sim arrives in town with the clothes on their back, a patch of bare dirt, and not a single Simoleon to their name. No job will save them — only their own two hands. This is the challenge that started a thousand simmers actually using the painting easel, and it's the gateway drug to every harder run in this book.*

| | |
|:--|:--|
| **Type** | Rags to Riches |
| **Difficulty** | ◆◆◆◇◇ &nbsp; Hard (3/5) |
| **Length** | Multi-session (one Sim's lifetime is plenty) |
| **Best for** | Players bored of starting rich; anyone learning the money skills |
| **Packs required** | Base game only |
| **Packs recommended** | Seasons (weather adds real stakes), Eco Lifestyle (off-grid options) |

**The goal** — Build your household funds up to **§100,000**, with every Simoleon earned from skills and odd jobs rather than a paid (rabbit-hole) career.

**Getting started**

Create one young adult Sim with any traits — though *Frugal*, *Hardworking*-style aspirations and anything that boosts a money skill will help. Move them onto the largest cheap empty lot you can find, then set up the bare start:

1. Open the console (Ctrl + Shift + C) and type `testingcheats true`.
2. Type `freerealestate on`, place your Sim on the empty lot, then type `freerealestate off`.
3. Type `money 0`.

| Setup | Detail |
|:--|:--|
| **Lot** | Empty, 20×15 or larger, cheapest you can find |
| **Funds** | §0 |
| **Aging** | On, normal lifespan |
| **Sims** | One young adult; no starting furniture or shelter |

**General rules**
1. No money cheats after the §0 start. `bb.moveobjects` for building is fine.
2. Only buy what you can currently afford — no going into a negative balance via tricks.
3. No gifts, transfers, or money from any other household.

**Specific rules**
1. No rabbit-hole career income. Money comes only from skills and self-employment — painting, writing, gardening, fishing, woodworking, crafting, collecting.
2. You begin with no walls and no roof; build your shelter up from open sky as you can afford it.
3. No moving into a pre-furnished house to skip the early grind.

**Strategy tips**
- *Fishing and collecting* are your day-one lifeline — they cost nothing and the frogs/crystals/fish sell immediately. Fish near the starting pond while your other skills build.
- *The painting easel* is the classic engine: buy the cheapest one the moment you can, and paint constantly. Painting skill raises the value of every canvas, so early grind compounds fast.
- Plant anything you find foraging — a free garden is passive income that grows while you sleep.

**Scoring (optional)** — Race friends by *in-game days to §100,000*: under 40 days is strong, under 25 is expert. Lower is better.

**Want it harder**
- *One-crop start:* every Simoleon must trace back to a single starting plant before you may diversify.
- *Off-grid:* enable the Off-the-Grid lot trait so you have no power or water to lean on either.

**Milestones**
- [ ] First §1,000 earned
- [ ] One-room shelter (bed, toilet, fridge, shower)
- [ ] Level 7 in a money-making skill
- [ ] §20,000 net worth
- [ ] Reach §100,000

**Variations** — Run it as a *family* rags-to-riches (start a couple with kids at §0), or set the target to §250,000 for a full-lifetime version.

### 2. From Tenant to Tycoon

*You start the month scraping together rent for a single cramped unit. You end it as the landlord every other tenant answers to. This is the rags-to-riches climb reimagined for the For Rent era — a slow, satisfying rise from paying rent to collecting it.*

| | |
|:--|:--|
| **Type** | Rags to Riches · Build · Property |
| **Difficulty** | ◆◆◆◇◇ &nbsp; Hard (3/5) |
| **Length** | Multi-session |
| **Best for** | Players who love management and property over the painting grind |
| **Packs required** | For Rent |
| **Packs recommended** | City Living, Get to Work |

**The goal** — Go from renting a single unit you can barely afford to **owning and running three profitable rental properties**.

**Getting started**

Create one ambitious young adult. Rather than buying a home, rent the cheapest available unit in a residential rental lot. Set funds low so the early squeeze is real:

1. `testingcheats true`
2. `money 800`

| Setup | Detail |
|:--|:--|
| **Lot** | Rent the cheapest unit you can find |
| **Funds** | §800 |
| **Aging** | On, normal |
| **Sims** | One tenant; a *Business Savvy* aspiration suits |

**General rules**
1. No money cheats after the §800 start.
2. Every Simoleon is earned — no transfers or gifts.
3. You must live as a tenant first; you can't buy a rental until you've paid rent honestly.

**Specific rules**
1. Pay your own rent on time, every cycle. Eviction is a fail.
2. Save toward your first rentable property; buy it only when you can afford it outright.
3. Every property you own must stay genuinely livable — handle repairs, pests, mould and tenant complaints.
4. Reinvest profits into the next property before upgrading your own lifestyle.

**Strategy tips**
- Keep your own unit cheap far longer than feels comfortable — every Simoleon you don't spend on yourself is a deposit on your first building.
- A small side income (handiness for your own repairs, a home-based skill) smooths the gap between buying a property and it turning profit.
- Screen for the cheapest run-down building first; low purchase price beats a pretty one when you're starting out.

**Scoring (optional)** — +1 point per fully-occupied rental month; aim for a long unbroken streak across all three buildings.

**Want it harder**
- *Slumlord to saint:* buy the most run-down building in town and renovate it to a five-star rental before buying a second.
- Forbid any personal income — you may only ever earn through rent.

**Milestones**
- [ ] Pay your first month's rent on time
- [ ] Save §10,000
- [ ] Buy your first rental property
- [ ] Keep a tenant happy for a full lease
- [ ] Own and run three properties

**Variations** — Play it as a *family business* where each grown child manages one building, or cap it at one perfect five-star property instead of three.

### 3. The One-Crop Empire

*Everything you will ever own grows from a single seed. One plant, chosen at the start, becomes the root of an entire fortune — every grafted branch, every bottle of nectar, every Simoleon traceable back to that first humble sprout.*

| **Packs recommended** | Seasons, Cottage Living, Eco Lifestyle |

**The goal** — Reach **§75,000** with every income stream rooted in one original plant and its descendants.

**Getting started**

Create one Sim with the *Freelance Botanist* aspiration or *Loves Outdoors* trait. Move onto an empty rural lot and set a small start:

1. `testingcheats true`
2. `money 500`

| Setup | Detail |
|:--|:--|
| **Lot** | Empty rural lot with growing space |
| **Funds** | §500 |
| **Aging** | On, normal |
| **Sims** | One gardener |

**General rules**
1. No money cheats after the §500 start.
2. All income is earned by your own household.
3. No transfers or gifts.

**Specific rules**
1. Buy or forage exactly **one** plant or seed to begin. Everything you grow must descend from it via harvest or graft.
2. Income comes only from that plant's produce, its grafts, or goods you craft from it (nectar, arrangements, preserves).
3. No careers, gigs, or unrelated collectibles for money.
4. Reinvest only into gardening and processing equipment.

**Strategy tips**
- Choose your founding plant carefully: a plant that *grafts well* lets you branch into new produce while staying within the rules.

**Scoring (optional)** — Track *fewest distinct purchases of outside seeds* (ideally exactly one). A true single-plant empire scores highest.

**Want it harder**
- Pick the lowest-value common plant available as your founder and still reach the target.
- Forbid grafting — the empire must run on one unmodified crop.

**Milestones**
- [ ] First harvest sold
- [ ] First successful graft
- [ ] §10,000 from produce
- [ ] Gardening skill maxed
- [ ] Reach §75,000

**Variations** — Make it generational: the founding plant is an heirloom passed down, and each generation must add one new grafted branch.

### 7. Debt to Diamonds

*Your Sim doesn't start at zero — they start below it. A §50,000 millstone of debt hangs over them from day one, and only once it's gone may they even think about the mansion they're determined to build, brick by mortgage-free brick.*

| **Packs recommended** | Get to Work, Seasons |

**The goal** — Clear a **§50,000 starting debt**, then build a mortgage-free mansion worth **§200,000+**.

**Getting started**

Create one earner. Move into a modest home, set funds to zero, and write your §50,000 debt on a tracker (a notes app or the in-game notebook):

1. `testingcheats true`
2. `money 0` (and record "Debt: §50,000")

| Setup | Detail |
|:--|:--|
| **Lot** | Modest existing home |
| **Funds** | §0, with a tracked §50,000 debt |
| **Aging** | On, normal |
| **Sims** | One earner |

**General rules**
1. No money cheats after the §0 start.
2. All income earned by your household.
3. No gifts or transfers.

## 2. Family & Generational

*The long game. Found a bloodline and carry it across generations, passing the household — and its rules, curses and ambitions — from heir to heir. Patience and planning matter more than any single Sim here; you're playing for the dynasty, not the day. This category is the home of the genre-defining classics — the Legacy Challenge, the 100 Baby Challenge and Not So Berry — presented with their real, creator-credited rules.*

### 1. The Legacy Challenge

*The one that started it all. A single founder steps onto a bare lot with nothing but the clothes they're wearing, and ten generations later their descendants still live on that exact spot — a family tree grown from dirt into a dynasty. It's less a challenge than a way of life, and almost every other challenge in this category is its child.*

| | |
|:--|:--|
| **Type** | Legacy |
| **Difficulty** | ◆◆◆◆◇ &nbsp; Brutal (4/5) |
| **Length** | 10 generations |
| **Best for** | Players who want a long-haul, multi-generational anchor save |
| **Packs required** | Base game only |
| **Packs recommended** | Parenthood, Seasons, Cottage Living, Growing Together |

**The goal** — Take a single bloodline through **ten unbroken generations**, always living on the founder's original lot.

**Getting started**

Create one young-adult founder with any traits and aspiration. Move them onto a large empty lot (the classic is a 50×50), and play with no money cheats from that point on.

1. `testingcheats true`
2. `freerealestate on` → place the founder on the empty lot → `freerealestate off`
3. Build and furnish only what the founder can afford — **no money cheats ever again** (`bb.moveobjects` for building is fine).

| Setup | Detail |
|:--|:--|
| **Lot** | Largest empty lot you can find (50×50 classic) |
| **Funds** | Starting funds only; never topped up by cheats |
| **Aging** | On, normal |
| **Sims** | One founder, nothing but themselves |

**Choose your succession laws.** Before you begin, pick how each generation's heir is decided — this is the heart of the challenge:
- **Gender Law:** Patriarchy · Matriarchy · Equality · Strict Equality.
- **Bloodline Law:** Strict Traditional (heir must be born in) · Traditional · Modern (adopted heirs allowed) · Foster.
- **Heir Law:** First Born · Last Born · Living Will · Merit · Strict · Random · Exemplar · Democracy.
- **Species Law** (if you allow occults): how occult heirs are treated.

**General rules**
1. No money cheats, ever, after setup.
2. The family never moves — every generation lives and dies on the founding lot.
3. Each generation has exactly one named heir who inherits the lot and bloodline.

**Specific rules**
1. Heirs are produced according to your chosen succession laws — no swapping laws mid-run to suit you.
2. Only the current heir's children can continue the line.
3. The line must survive ten full generations; an heir dying with no successor ends the legacy.

**Strategy tips**
- Always have the *next* heir secured before an heir reaches elder — a sudden death with no successor is the classic legacy-ender.
- Parenthood's character values make raising capable heirs far easier; lean on it.
- Build vertically and in phases — you'll be adding rooms for a growing family for ten generations, so leave space.

**Scoring (optional)** — The Legacy Challenge has a long-standing optional points system. Award points across categories as the family achieves them: each **generation** completed, **aspirations** completed, **skills** maxed, **careers** topped, **collections** finished, and special feats (e.g. a member reaching the top of a difficult skill or hosting a perfect event). Tally your dynasty's lifetime score at Generation 10. Treat it as a personal high score to beat on your next run.

**Want it harder**
- Pick the strictest succession laws (Strict Traditional bloodline + Strict heir law) and never break them, even when the "correct" heir is your worst Sim.
- Combine with a §0, off-grid founder for a true from-nothing dynasty.

**Milestones**
- [ ] Establish the founder and choose your laws
- [ ] Produce a Generation 2 heir
- [ ] Reach Generation 5
- [ ] Reach Generation 8
- [ ] Crown the Generation 10 heir

**Variations** — Theme the dynasty (a family of artists, of villains, of occults), or layer Not So Berry's colour rules on top for a themed legacy.

<sub>**Credit & history:** The Legacy Challenge was created by **Pinstar**, carried over from earlier Sims games and maintained for The Sims 4; the succession-law framework is the community standard. &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Last tested:** Patch 1.108 — Feb 2026</sub>

### 2. The 100 Baby Challenge

*One mother. One hundred children. As few generations as humanly (or in-game-ly) possible. It's the most infamous challenge in the game for a reason: an endurance test of nappies, donors and sheer logistics where the matriarch does everything herself and the babies never stop coming.*

| | |
|:--|:--|
| **Type** | Legacy · Endurance |
| **Difficulty** | ◆◆◆◆◆ &nbsp; Extreme (5/5) |
| **Length** | Open-ended (until the 100th birth) |
| **Best for** | Players who want a marathon and love chaos |
| **Packs required** | Base game only |
| **Packs recommended** | Get to Work / City Living (work-from-home income), Parenthood, Growing Together |

**The goal** — Give birth to **100 children** using as **few matriarchs as possible**. The run ends the instant the 100th child is born — or the instant you break a rule.

**Getting started**

Create one young-adult matriarch who can get pregnant (any gender customisation that allows pregnancy; any traits/aspiration). Move her into any home she can afford. Turn **NPC auto-aging on** so the town keeps generating fresh donors.

1. `testingcheats true` (for setup/glitch-fixes only)
2. Move in on starting funds — **no money cheats**; `bb.moveobjects` for building is allowed.

| Setup | Detail |
|:--|:--|
| **Lot** | Any home affordable on starting funds; move later only if affordable |
| **Funds** | Starting funds only |
| **Aging** | On, normal; NPC auto-aging on |
| **Sims** | One matriarch |

**General rules**
1. **No cheats** except `bb.moveobjects` (building) and resolving glitches. No money cheats; no mods that raise household size or pay child support.
2. Aging on, normal lifespan.
3. Raise every child properly — you may never make room by neglect, death, or letting Social Services take a child.

**Specific rules (the canonical ruleset)**
1. **One donor, one child.** A Sim who has fathered one of your children can never be used again — every child has a different father. Donors give **no** help: they don't move in, mind children, or provide money.
2. The matriarch may only work a **work-from-home / freelance / self-employed** job — no rabbit-hole career, no part-time. Teens may take part-time jobs to help.
3. **No nanny or babysitter, ever.** Maids, gardeners and repair techs are allowed but must not do childcare; a butler must have childcare disabled.
4. Children's traits are set by the **in-game randomiser** (you may pick aspirations to match). You may not influence a baby's gender.
5. The matriarch can't marry while of child-bearing age; she may marry as an elder but must move out with her spouse.
6. **Aging-up gates** (no rushing): newborns only on their birthday notification; toddlers at level 3 in all toddler skills; children/teens only with an A in school (plus a character value if you have Parenthood). Household holds a maximum of **seven children at a time**.
7. **Succession:** when the matriarch becomes an elder and can no longer bear children, her **youngest daughter** becomes the next matriarch (you may move the elder out once that daughter is a young adult).

**Strategy tips**
- The matriarch's income lifeline is selling **paintings, books or garden produce** from home — set one up early, because she literally cannot hold a normal job.
- Chase **multiples**: the *Fertile* reward trait, the *On a Ley Line* lot trait, and the Wellness "Fertility Massage" all raise your odds of twins and triplets — each multiple birth is two or three babies toward 100 from a single pregnancy, and fewer pregnancies means fewer matriarchs.
- Keep a written list of used donors so you never accidentally repeat one (an instant rule-break).

**Scoring (optional)** — Your score is about **efficiency**: the fewer matriarchs (generations) you use to reach 100, the better. A single-matriarch run is near-impossible; two or three is an excellent result. The earlier "Cheesecake" ancestor of this challenge aimed for 100 in 50 births or fewer.

**Want it harder**
- **Single-matriarch run:** reach 100 with only one mother, leaning entirely on multiples.
- Forbid maids and all hired help — the matriarch does every chore as well as every nappy.

**Milestones**
- [ ] Survive your first batch of toddlers
- [ ] Reach baby #25
- [ ] Pass the title to a second matriarch (or push past where most need to)
- [ ] Reach baby #50
- [ ] Welcome baby #100

**Variations** — The "**no-help hard mode**" (no maids at all), or a themed version where every child is named alphabetically or by birth order.

<sub>**Credit & history:** A community classic descended from the Sims 2 "Cheesecake Challenge"; the modern, regularly-updated official ruleset is maintained by **Snarky Sims (Snarky Sims Witch)**. &nbsp;·&nbsp; **Last tested:** Patch 1.108 — Feb 2026</sub>

### 1. The Decades Challenge

*Begin in gaslit 1890, and march your bloodline forward one decade at a time — through wars, women's suffrage, the arrival of electricity and the telephone, all the way to the present day. **The goal** — Live from the **1890s to the present day**, one decade per generation, following era-accurate rules for each.

**Getting started**

Create one young-adult founder or a married young-adult couple (you may add a parent for an authentic multi-generational 1890s home). Use only your starting funds.

1. `testingcheats true`
2. Money cheats are allowed **only as a repayable loan** — pay it back, plus 10% interest, before your founders die.

| Setup | Detail |
|:--|:--|
| **Lot** | A period-appropriate home |
| **Funds** | Starting funds (or a repaid "loan") |
| **Aging** | On, normal until the 1950s, then long |
| **Sims** | One founder or a married couple |

**A note on the era rules:** the early decades include restrictions around gender, class and race that mirror the real history of the period. The challenge's creators are explicit that it **does not condone** those limitations — they exist to dramatise how much changed over a century, and players are openly encouraged to adapt or drop any rule they're not comfortable with.

**General rules**
1. Each decade has its own rules on careers, aspirations, clothing, objects and technology — research and honour them.
2. No cheats except the repayable starter loan.
3. Advance one decade per generation.

**Specific rules (the real mechanics)**
1. **Technology gating:** no electricity in the 1890s (candles and lanterns); cell phones and computers are heavily restricted until the decades they'd actually exist (a computer may stand in for a "typewriter" for writing only).
2. **The wars:** for WWI (1910s), WWII, Korea and Vietnam, eligible Sims are "sent to war" — simulated by feeding them a slice of **Cowplant** cake and rolling a die (odds = they die, evens = they return, traumatised, gaining a negative trait). Grow your Cowplant before 1910.
3. **Era-specific life rules** (inheritance, who may work, who may marry) change each decade to reflect the period — adapt them to your comfort.

**Strategy tips**
- Grow a healthy Cowplant *well* before 1910 — the war mechanic depends on it, and a hungry household can lose it.
- Tackle one decade's custom content and rules at a time rather than prepping all fourteen at once.
- Use the **likes/dislikes** system to stop Sims autonomously using anachronistic objects (set a 1920s Sim to dislike gaming, for instance).

**Want it harder**
- Play one of the **extended** versions that begin in the 1700s (or even the 1300s) with extra historical death-rolls.
- Track exact in-game years and age Sims up precisely to the calendar.

**Milestones**
- [ ] Establish the 1890s founding family
- [ ] Survive the First World War
- [ ] Reach the 1950s
- [ ] Reach the 1980s
- [ ] Arrive in the present day
